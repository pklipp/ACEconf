A Darwinian Perspective on Agile Architecture Agile Architecture

with Mina Boström Nakićenović

In 1859 Darwin published a book ”Origin of the Species”, with a lot of evolutionary ideas. One of his main contributions to the theory of evolution was a plausible mechanism called "natural selection". There are always variations within a species. But some advantageous variations allow members of the species to survive better than others. Darwin concluded: ”It is not the strongest of the species that survive, nor the most intelligent; it is the one most adaptable to change.” Does it sound familiar? From your experience, which software systems usually survive best: the technically perfect one or the one most responsive and adaptable to change?

Let’s consider software systems as ”species”, and let’s try to apply some of the Darwin’s principles when developing them. What can we get? Can we, somehow, discover the right variation, i.e. “genotype” of the system, which could guarantee its survival on the tough business market?

Evolution of natural world lasts billions of years while in software development we usually have a much shorter time-to market. Therefore we do not have billions of years to discover the right “genotype” of our software systems as the “mother nature” does. But we can use similar principles and mechanisms! Can we use the Lean Startup’s ”MVP” concept and the ”pivot or persevere” strategy as a catalyzer in our experimentation and innovation processes? Which mechanisms should we use in order to get our software systems to be not only robust, or resilient but also antifragile?

If you are interested in finding answers on these questions welcome to my talk! Through the comparison of the Darwin’s theory of evolution to software development, we will try to find an answer to how to build sustainable, agile and antifragile software systems, which are responsive and adaptable to the challenges of a volatile business environments.

Mina's Bio:

Mina is a passionate and certified IT architect with 18 years of experience of software development. The latest 10 years she has been working on high-performance financial market systems at SunGard Front Arena, Stockholm. With a Serbian persistence and a Swedish practicality Mina achieves to find lightweight, agile architectural solutions, delivered on time and within budget.

She was a speaker on several industry conferences, such as Agile Lean Europe 2015, Optional Conference 2015, Agile Lean Europe 2014, GeeCON 2014, Agile Prague 2012, ICSEA 2011 and CEE-SECR 2010. She holds a MSc in Electrical Engineering and a Ph Lic in Computer Science.
