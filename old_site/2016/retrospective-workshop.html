Retrospective workshop

with Luis Gonçalves and Marc Löffler

Organizations need to improve to stay in business and keep delivering value. Classical organizational improvement using large programs takes too long and is often inefficient and ineffective. We need to uncover better ways to improve. Thus, retrospectives can provide solution. Agile retrospectives are a great way to continuously improve the way of working. Getting feasible actions out of a retrospective and getting them done helps teams to learn and improve. The agile manifesto proposes that a “team reflects on how to become more effective”. Agile retrospectives can be used by teams to inspect and adapt their way of working.

Who should participate?

Agile Coaches, Scrum Masters, Team Facilitators, Managers/Leaders, anyone running retrospectives

Workshop learning goals

  • Business Value of Agile Retrospectives
  • How to create a safe environment to run a Retrospective
  • Different exercises for Agile Retrospectives
  • Different phases of a team - exercises
  • How to “push” teams to a different level
