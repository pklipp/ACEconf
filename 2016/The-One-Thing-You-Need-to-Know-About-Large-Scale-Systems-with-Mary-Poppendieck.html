The One Thing You Need to Know About Large-Scale Systems with Mary Poppendieck


Two decades ago, a new generation of software engineers discovered that the Internet created explosive potential for growth. The sheer scale of what might be possible inspired these engineers to rethink and reinvent the way to approach really tough problems presented by massive scale. When it became impossible to get any bigger, they figured out how to get small things to work at scale. When data grew so large that it no longer could be fit onto one computer, they figured out how to distribute data effectively. When release cycles grew from months to years, they discovered how to safely deliver software continuously. When complexity threatened to bring down every large system, they figured out how to make internet-scale systems more reliable than ever.

When size explodes or market forces take a sudden unexpected turn, the impossible suddenly becomes necessary for survival. This talk is about what’s important – and what’s not – when you absolutely, positively MUST get those large scale systems working. Now.
